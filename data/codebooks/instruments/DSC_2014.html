Component Description

The Discrimination section (DSC) provides specific information relating to how the respondent has been treated during his/her life.Â  Answers to questions regarding unfair treatment related to race, age, gender, culture, religion, physical appearance, and sexual orientation are recorded.

Eligible Sample

SHOW participants ages 18+ are eligible for this section.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

The DSC section was included in a booklet of self-administered questionnaires provided to study participants at the end of the first in home interview to complete and return to SHOW.

Data Processing

The DSC section data were systematically reviewed for logical, processing, and data input errors during the process of data collection and entry by SHOW staff.

Analytic Notes

Responses for questions DSC020 have "Don't know" coded as nonmissing; for all other questions, "Don't know" is coded as .D. These data were collected as part of the self-administered portion of the SHOW study and SAQ sample weights should be used when analyzing these data. In the 2014, 30 participants did not follow the skip pattern at the end of DSC010_H. They answered "No" to all questions DSC010_A through DSC010_H, but then answered at least one question from DSC020 to DSC050. All data was left in the final dataset.

References

Williams, David R., et al. "Racial Differences in Physical and Mental Health Socio-economic Status, Stress and Discrimination." Journal of Health Psychology 2.3 (1997): 335-351.
