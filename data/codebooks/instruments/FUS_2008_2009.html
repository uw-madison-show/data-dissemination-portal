Component Description

The SHOW Follow-up Survey provides longitudinal information on SHOW participants. The following SHOW questionnaires were modified for use in the follow-up survey: Housing Characteristics; Health History; Screening and Immunization; Insurance, Access, and Utilization; Occupation; Physical; Your Health (SF-12); Characteristics of Your Neighborhood; Smoking; Food Security; Weight History; PHQ-8; Demographics; Tracking. A small number of questions were added to explore general life event and health changes since last contact.

Eligible Sample

SHOW participants from the 2008-2009 cohort were eligible for this follow-up survey.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

The SHOW Follow-up Survey was conducted by Computer-Assisted Telephone Interviewing, managed by the UW Survey Center. UWSC interviewers called participants and completed data collection over the phone. Interviews lasted about 15-30 minutes.

Data Processing

The Follow-up Survey data were systematically reviewed for logical, processing, and data input errors during the process of data collection and entry by the University of Wisconsin Survey Center. Further data processing and cleaning were done b y staff of the Survey of the Health of Wisconsin.

Analytic Notes

Of SHOW core questions included in the Follow-up Survey, the majority were included exactly as originally asked. A small number were edited so tense was appropriate and temporal relationships could be assessed. As mentioned above, some new ques tions were written and added. Information about edited and new questions can be obtained from SHOW for use in data analyses. Researchers should obtain this information before comparing SHOW core and follow-up data.Â  Follow-up survey sampling weights should be used when analyzing these data. Sampling weights are currently in development.
