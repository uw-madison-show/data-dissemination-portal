Component Description

The Housing Characteristics questionnaire provides specific information relating to the physical home environment.

Eligible Sample

SHOW participants ages 21-74 are eligible for this section. From June to November of 2008, only one recipient from each household completed the Household Characteristics questionnaire, selected non-randomly by the participants within the household. From December 2008 onward, the questionnaire was administered to all participants in the household.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

Information about the dwelling characteristics was obtained by trained interviewers during the household interview.  In 2008, an interviewer administered paper and pencil interview (PAPI) was used.  In 2009, a computer-assisted personal interview (CAPI) was used.

Data Processing and Editing

The Housing Characteristics questionnaire data were reviewed for logical, processing, and data input errors during data collection. The CAPI system is programmed with automated data consistency checks to reduce data entry errors. 

Known Analytic Notes

Radon testing (HOQ.066.20): There may be an inconsistency in the skip pattern for this question in 2008-9 PAPI versus 2010 CAPI versions.  The UWSC laboratory test instrument for 2011 skips HOQ.066.20 and HOQ.066.21 (results of radon testing) if the respondent answers, “No” to item HOQ.066 (“Do you have a basement in this home?”.  This is not the skip pattern specified in writing on the PAPI and CAPI questionnaire documents.

Weed killers/insecticides (HOQ.260.020, HOQ260.040): Different paper versions were used for this section. In some versions, the question regarding who applied the chemicals has different responses for value 4 (either “A landlord” or “Some other person or company”). For the method of application, some versions had Bomb 2, Powder 3, Strip 4, Other 5 while others had Powder 2, Strip 3, Other 4. Responses from these questions have not been evaluated for accuracy.

Pesticides (HOQ.265.020): Different paper versions were used for this section. In some versions, the question regarding who applied the chemicals has different responses for value 4 (either “A landlord” or “Some other person or company”). Responses from these questions have not been evaluated for accuracy.
