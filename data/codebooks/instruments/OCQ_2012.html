Title: Occupation (OCQ)

Years of Content: 2012

First Published: March 2013

Last Revised: March 2014

Subject: Occupation History

Record Source(s): SHOW original and questions modified from NHANES and the Wisconsin School Study Project (1975)

Data File: ocq_2012.sas7bdat

Component Description

The occupation (OCQ) section asks questions about the respondent’s work experience. This section goes into details on the type of work that was done and duties performed so that this information can be accurately categorized into occupations.

Eligible Sample

SHOW participants ages 21-74 are eligible for this section.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

The OCQ section was completed during the first interview of a subject at the subject’s home by a trained interviewer using computer-assisted personal interview (CAPI) form January 2012 to December 2012. Trained interviewers used active probes to get a clear picture of what they did on this job so trained coders can assign "standard industry and occupation codes".

Data Processing

OCQ section data were systematically reviewed for logical, processing, and data input errors during the process of data collection and entry by the University of Wisconsin Survey Center. Further data processing and cleaning were done by staff of the Survey of the Health of Wisconsin.

Analytic Notes

These data were collected as part of the in-home personal interview portion of the SHOW study (Time 1), and T1 sample weights should be used when analyzing these data. OCQ14010, OCQ14020, OCQ21010 OCQ21020 were added on 7/24/2012. Therefore, participants who were interviewed before this date have missing values for these questions.
