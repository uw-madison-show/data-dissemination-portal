Subject: Stress, Anxiety, and Depression

Record Source(s): Jackson Heart Study (Stress, STS); DASS

Data File: qe_2013.sas7bdat

Component Description

The QE_STRESS section measures how often people have feelings and behaviors that might indicate stress, anxiety, and depression. The first part of the questionnaire is based on the Global Perceived Stress Scale (STS), an 8-item self-report measure of perceived chronic stress, [originally] created for use in the Jackson Heart Study. The second part of the questionnaire is based on the 42-item Depression and Anxiety Stress Scales (DASS), a set of three self-report scales [14 items each] designed to measure the negative emotional states of depression, anxiety, and stress.

Eligible Sample

SHOW participants ages 21-74 are eligible for this section.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

The QE_STRESS section was included in a booklet of self-administered questionnaires provided to study participants at the end of the first in home interview to complete and return to SHOW.

Data Processing

The QE_STRESS section data were systematically reviewed for logical, processing, and data input errors during the process of data collection and entry by the Survey of the Health of Wisconsin.

Analytic Notes

These data were collected as part of the self-administered portion of the SHOW study (Time 2) and T2 sample weights should be used when analyzing these data.

References

Lovibond, S.H. & Lovibond, P.F. (1995). Manual for the Depression Anxiety Stress Scales. (2nd. Ed.) Sydney: Psychology Foundation.
