LOCATOR RECORD

Title: Short Test of Funcational Health Literacy in Adults (STOFHLA)

Years of Content: 2008-2009

First Published: March 2011

Last Revised: N/A

Access and Use Constraints: None

Subject: Health Literacy

Record Source: Patient Education and Counseling, 38, 33-42

Data File: stofhla_2008_2009.sas7bdat

Component Description

STOFHLA tests a patient’s ability to read passages using real materials from the health care setting.  Pages are selected from instructions for preparation for an upper GI series and the patient righsts and responsibilities section of a Medicaid application form. 

Eligible Sample

SHOW participants ages 21-74 are eligible for this section.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

The STOFHLA section is completed during the Time 3 (survey center) visit. It is self-administered by the respondents using photocopied masters developemet and published by Peppercorn Books & Press Inc.

Data Processing and Editing

The STOFHLA section data were systematically reviewed for logical, processing, and data input errors by staff at the Survey of the Health of Wisconsin.

Analytic Notes

These data were collected as part of the Time 3 portion of the SHOW study T3 sample weights should be used when analyzing these data.

 

Some variables are not included in the data set. These include variables that denote specific quality control measures.  These variables are available upon request.

References

Baker DW, Parker RM, Williams MV, Gazmarian JA, Nurss JR (1999). Development of a brief test to measure functional health literacy.  Patient Education and Counseling, 38, 22-42.
