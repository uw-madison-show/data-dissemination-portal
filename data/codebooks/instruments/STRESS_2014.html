Title: Stress

Subject: Stress, Anxiety, and Depression

Record Source(s): Jackson Heart Study; DASS

Data File: stress_2014.sas7bdat

Component Description

The STRESS section measures how often people have feelings and behaviors that might indicate stress, anxiety, and depression. The first part of the questionnaire is based on the Global Perceived Stress Scale, an 8-item self-report measure of perceived chronic stress, originally created for use in the Jackson Heart Study. The second STRESS section measures how often people have feelings and behaviors that might indicate stress, anxiety, and depression.

Eligible Sample

SHOW participants ages 18+ are eligible for this section.

Interview Setting and Mode of Administration

The STRESS section was included in a booklet of self-administered questionnaires provided to study participants at the end of the first in home interview to complete and return to SHOW.

Data Processing

The STRESS section data were systematically reviewed for logical, processing, and data input errors during the process of data collection and entry by the Survey of the Health of Wisconsin.

Analytic Notes

The SHOW study used the DASS long version (42 item questionnaire) from 2008-2013. Starting in 2014, the DASS short version (21 item questionnaire) was used. These data were collected as part of the self-administered portion of the SHOW study and sample weights should be used when analyzing these data (see the Analytic Guidelines for information on what weights to use).

References

Henry, J.D. & Crawford, J.R. The short-form version of the Depression Anxiety Stress Scales (DASS-21): construct validity and normative data in a large non-clinical sample. The British journal of Clinical Psychology / the British Psychological Society 2005; 44: 227-239.
