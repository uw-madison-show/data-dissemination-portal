Component Description

Walk Score®, Bike Score®, and Transit Score® results were calculated from the Walk Score website (http://www.walkscore.com) based on respondent addresses.

Analytic Notes

Bike Score and Transit Score results are not available for all addresses. Details of the methodology used to create these scores can be found on the Walk Score website (http://www.walkscore.com). Scores were submitted on November 1, 2013 and are subject to change based on updates to establishments recognized by Walk Score. Results may not be comparable to previous years due to changes in scoring algorithm used by Walk Score, where previous cohorts used a "Street Smart" walk score. All addresses were sent to Walk Score; if the address was determined to be "car-dependent," a walk score of 0 was assigned. If results were not provided for bike score or transit score, missing values were recorded.

All derived variables in this dataset used variables collected during the Time 1 portion of the SHOW study and T1 survey weights should be used when analyzing these data. All processing was completed using SAS version 9.3. Short hand SAS calculations have been included to help researchers recreate these variables from the raw data. Additional details are available upon request.

WALK - WALK SCORE

Variable Name WALK
Variable Format FMT_NUMERIC
English Text Walk score calculated from Walk Score® (http://www.walkscore.com/).
English Instructions Walk Score measures the walkability of any address using a patent-pending system. For each address, Walk Score analyzes hundreds of walking routes to nearby amenities. Points are awarded based on the distance to the amenities in each category. Amenities within a 5 minute walk (.25 miles) are awarded maximum points. For larger areas such as cities or neighborhoods, we calculate a population-weighted score.

English Instructions Bike Score measures whether an area is good for biking. For a given location, a Bike Score is calculated by measuring bike infrastructure (lanes, trails, etc.), hills, destinations and road connectivity, and the number of bike commuters.
